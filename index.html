Top 3 Tourist Attractions In China

Planning a vacation to China, but don't know where to start?


#1: Great Wall of China in Beijing

The Great Wall of China is one of China's most well-known tourist attractions. Built as a military defensive fort back in the 17th century AD, this wall took over 2,000 years to build and now stretches over 20,000 km across the northern border of China. The Great Wall of China consists of walla, watch towers, fortresses, etc. For anyone who decides to go here, be prepared for a long hike. Bring plenty of waters, foods, and sunscreen!


#2: Chengdu Panda Base in Sichuan

For panda lovers, this will be the highlight of your vacation. In Sichuan, one of the largest tourist attractions is the Panda Base, also known as the Chengdu Panda Research Base. The Panda Base in Sichuan started with 6 giant pandas rescued fro mthe wild. As the pandas gave, birth the once endangered population no longer bears that title. With the help of this research base, China's now has more than 1,800 pandas and the number continues to grow. Meanwhile, as the Panda Base, lives 150 GIANT pandas that visitors and tourists are free to visit and interact with! To learn more about Chengdu Panda Base, its mission statement, and operations hours/logistics, please visit: Chengdu Panda Research Base


#3: Wuzhen Old City

Similar to the Venice Canals, the Wuzhen Old City has its own scenic canal with stone bridges going across and canoes transporting goods and visitors. This ancient city is over 1,000 years old and consists of eye-catching architecture with wooden buildings that have all sorts of Chinese history carved all over them. This attraction is great for the day, but even nicer at night when all the lanterns and colored lights turn on. Visiting during certain seasons and holidays may even introduce you to different festivals hosted by the city's residents.
